Welcome to Jackson!

What is Jackson?
Jackson is a small town and village in southeastern Wisconsin. It is located in Washington County, just a half hour drive north from Milwaukee. As of 2024, Jackson has a population of 8,102 and 34.4 square miles of land. Since its foundation in 1846, Jackson has grown exponentially in size and population as it has shifted from a farming town to a residential area.
Why You Should Visit
While not very big, Jackson has a couple interesting features to visit. Jackson is home to the Jackson Marsh Wildlife Area, a 2,600 acre piece of land controlled by the Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources. Here, you may hunt or do other outdoor activies when out of the hunting season. Jackson contains Jackson Park and Hickory Lane Park, suitable places to take your dog for a walk or relax with your family. On June 6 and 7, Action in Jackson will take place, a fun event for people of all ages. Come to Jackson today and enjoy the nice, pleasant atmosphere present in our town.
