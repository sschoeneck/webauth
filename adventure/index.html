Tour of the Solar System

January 11, 2024

Today we have set off from Earth to go on a journy around our solar system. I'm a bit nervous but excited for the scientific discoveries that will be made along the way. Before we set off, I triple checked we had all our important supllies on the ship. Some such supplies were:

  • Spacesuits
  • Food/Water
  • Scientific Instruments
  • Entertainment
  • Repair Supplies

This is sure to be some adventure. I've already taken some photos of Earth.

August 20, 2024

We have reached the Sun. The goal is to slingshot around it and launch ourselves far into the solar system. The faculae sure are blinding being this close to the sun. I'm excited to carry on past it though. I've ranked the various celestial bodies we will visit in order from most excited to least excited.

  1. Saturn
  2. Jupiter
  3. Mars
  4. Uranus
  5. Venus
  6. Earth
  7. Mercury
  8. Moon
  9. Neptune
  10. Sun

I can't wait to view the Kirkwood Gaps. Also to see first hand & know if there really is an Oort Cloud would be amazing.

Faculae
-Bright patches that are visible on the Sun's surface, or photosphere
Kirkwood Gaps
-Regions in the main belt of asteroids where few or no asteroids are found
Oort Cloud
-A theoretical shell of comets that is believed to exist at the outermost regions of our solar system

Here's a photo of the Sun I took.

November 21, 2025

We've finally reached Saturn, the celestial body I was looking forward to the most. I can't believe it's been about 22 months since we left Earth. It feels like it was only yesterday. I captured this beautiful picture of Saturn yesterday.
